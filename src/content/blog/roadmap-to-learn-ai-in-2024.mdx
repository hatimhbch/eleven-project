## Introduction

The field of Artificial Intelligence (AI) is rapidly evolving, offering exciting opportunities for programmers, hackers, and aspiring AI enthusiasts. Whether you're a complete beginner or have some foundational knowledge, this comprehensive roadmap is designed to guide you through the journey of mastering AI in 2024.

This article takes a top-down, code-first approach, emphasizing hands-on learning, project-based work, and active engagement with the AI community. By the end of this roadmap, you'll have the skills and knowledge to tackle real-world AI problems, build impressive projects, and potentially land your dream job in the AI industry.

## Mathematics: The Foundation of AI

Mastering the mathematical foundations of AI is crucial for understanding and implementing algorithms effectively. This roadmap focuses on three key pillars of mathematics: linear algebra, calculus, and probability/statistics.

### Linear Algebra

Linear algebra is the backbone of data representation and manipulation in AI. It provides the mathematical toolkit for working with matrices, vectors, and linear transformations. Start with the following resources:

- [Math for Machine Learning by Weights & Biases](https://www.wandb.com/articles/math-for-machine-learning)   (code-based approach)
- [Computational Linear Algebra by fast.ai](https://www.fast.ai/2017/07/17/num-lin-alg/)   (video and code)
- [Introduction to Linear Algebra for Applied Machine Learning with Python](https://github.com/fastai/numerical-linear-algebra)
- [Linear Algebra & Multivariate Calculus lectures by Imperial College London](https://www.imperial.ac.uk/computing/current-students/courses/linear-algebra-and-multivariate-calculus/)
- [3Blue1Brown's Essence of Linear Algebra](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL0-GT3co4r2y2YErbmuJw2L5tW4Ew2O5B)

### Calculus

Calculus is the engine for optimization in machine learning, enabling algorithms to learn and improve by understanding gradients and rates of change. Dive into the following resources:

- [3Blue1Brown's Essence of Calculus](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL0-GT3co4r2wlh6UHTUeQsrf3mlS2lk6x)
- [Math for Machine Learning by Weights & Biases](https://www.wandb.com/articles/math-for-machine-learning)   (code-based approach)

### Probability and Statistics

Probability and statistics form the foundation for decision-making under uncertainty, allowing algorithms to predict outcomes and learn from data through models of randomness and variability. Explore the following resources:

- [Statistics Fundamentals by StatQuest](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLblh5JKOoLUJo2Q6xK4tZElbIvn_jH8_i)
- [Math for Machine Learning by Weights & Biases](https://www.wandb.com/articles/math-for-machine-learning)   (code-based approach)

## Machine Learning: From Theory to Practice

### Foundational Knowledge

Begin your machine learning journey by reading the comprehensive [100-page Machine Learning book](https://www.cs.cornell.edu/courses/cs4780/2018fa/page/files/ml_book.pdf) . This will provide you with a solid theoretical foundation.

### Hands-on Experience

While reading the ML book, start implementing algorithms from scratch. This will deepen your understanding and prepare you for more advanced topics. Explore the following repositories:

- [ML-From-Scratch by Erik Linder-Norén](https://github.com/eriklindernoren/ML-From-Scratch)
- [Oracle by JeremyNixon](https://github.com/JeremyNixon/oracle)
- [Homemade Machine Learning by Oleksii Trekhleb](https://github.com/trekhleb/homemade-machine-learning)
- [MiniTorch: A DIY Course on Machine Learning Engineering](https://minitorch.github.io/)   (videos and code)

### Competitions and Side Projects

Apply your newfound knowledge by participating in machine learning competitions on platforms like [bitgrit](https://www.bitgrit.net/) and [Kaggle](https://www.kaggle.com/) . Study past winning solutions to learn from the best.

Additionally, work on side projects that involve building models and deploying them. Use resources like [earthaccess](https://earthaccess.nasa.gov/) to access NASA Earth data, and [Streamlit](https://streamlit.io/) to create user interfaces for your models.

### Deployment and Monitoring

Once you've built your models, learn how to deploy them and track their performance in production. Explore resources like:

- [Made With ML](https://madewithml.com/)
- [DataTalksClub/mlops-zoomcamp: Free MLOps course](https://github.com/DataTalksClub/mlops-zoomcamp)
- [chiphuyen/machine-learning-systems-design](https://github.com/chiphuyen/machine-learning-systems-design)
- [Evidently AI — ML system design: 300 case studies](https://evidentlyai.com/blog/ml-system-design-case-studies)
- [stas00/ml-engineering: Machine Learning Engineering Online Book](https://github.com/stas00/ml-engineering)
- [Machine Learning with PyTorch and Scikit-Learn by Sebastian Raschka and Vahid Mirjalili](https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/machine-learning-with/9781801072526/)   (code)
- [Introduction to Machine Learning Interviews by Chip Huyen](https://huyenchip.com/machine-learning-interviews-book/)

## Deep Learning: Mastering the Art of Neural Networks

### Top-down Approach

If you prefer a top-down, code-first approach to deep learning, start with the [fast.ai](https://www.fast.ai/) courses (part1 and part2) and join the [Weights & Biases Study Group](https://www.wandb.com/study-groups).

### Comprehensive Curriculum

For a more traditional, comprehensive deep learning course, check out the [UNIGE 14x050 — Deep Learning by François Fleuret](https://fleuret.org/unige-14x050/).

### Theoretical Foundations

As you progress, delve into the theoretical foundations of deep learning with the following resources:

- [Dive into Deep Learning](https://d2l.ai/)   (code examples in PyTorch, NumPy/MXNet, JAX, and TensorFlow)
- [Deep Learning by Ian Goodfellow, Yoshua Bengio, and Aaron Courville](https://www.deeplearningbook.org/)
- [Neural networks and deep learning](http://neuralnetworksanddeeplearning.com/)
- [Understanding Deep Learning (with hands-on notebooks)](https://github.com/https-deeplearning-ai/machine-learning-yearning-public)

### Competitions and Paper Implementations

Continue honing your deep learning skills by participating in competitions and implementing research papers:

- [PlantTraits2024 — FGVC11 | Kaggle](https://www.kaggle.com/competitions/planttraits2024-fgvc11)   (computer vision)
- [labml.ai Annotated PyTorch Paper Implementations](https://github.com/labmlai/annotated_deep_learning_paper_implementations)
- [Papers with Code](https://paperswithcode.com/)   (e.g., [BERT explained](https://paperswithcode.com/paper/bert-pre-training-of-deep-bidirectional)   )

## Staying Updated and Engaged

To stay up-to-date with the latest developments in AI and continue your learning journey, consider the following:

- **Learn in Public**   : Share your progress, insights, and projects on platforms like Twitter, LinkedIn, and personal blogs. This will help you connect with the AI community, create opportunities, and solidify your own understanding.
- **Follow AI Experts**   : Curate a list of influential AI researchers, engineers, and thought leaders on Twitter and engage with their content.
- **Participate in Hackathons and Competitions**   : Continuously challenge yourself by participating in AI-focused hackathons and competitions. This will expose you to real-world problems and help you develop practical skills.
- **Read Research Papers**   : Stay up-to-date with the latest advancements in AI by reading research papers published in reputable journals and conferences. Resources like [Papers with Code](https://paperswithcode.com/)   can help you discover and understand cutting-edge research.

## Conclusion

This comprehensive roadmap has provided you with a structured plan to learn AI in 2024. By mastering the foundational mathematics, tools, machine learning, deep learning, and large language models, you'll be well-equipped to tackle real-world AI problems, build impressive projects, and potentially land your dream job in the AI industry.

Remember, the journey of learning AI is never-ending, as the field continues to evolve rapidly. Stay curious, embrace a growth mindset, and don't be afraid to learn in public and engage with the AI community. Good luck on your AI learning adventure!
